Places to Visit in Ernakulam

Mangalavanam Bird Sanctuary

The Bird Sanctuary plays a significant role in balancing the ecology of Kochi, hence it is often regarded as the 'green lung of Kochi. This area is a roosting place for many kinds of resident and migratory birds. The sanctuary houses 41species of birds representing 25 families. The most common bird species found at Mangalavanam are common redshank, common greenshank, brahminy kite, white-breasted waterhen, and marsh sandpiper. Mammals in the sanctuary. Mammals such as flying-fox, painted bat, Eurasian otter.

Bolghatty Palace

The Palace was built in 1744 by the Dutch trader and was later taken over by the British. Today, the palace is a popular hotel run by the Kerala Tourism Development Corporation. The palace is two-storied containing well-decorated and a huge lounge, which exhibits historical portraits.The place can be reached by boat from the mainland Ernakulam. The Island is also home for a fine golf course amidst backwaters. At a distance of 4 km from Ernakulam Junction (South) Railway Station, The Palace is located on Bolgatty Island near Vallarpadam Island. towering cliffs.

Salim Ali Bird Sanctuary

There is a deer park inside the sanctuary. Adjacent to the deer park, the Taxidermic Museum showcases the natives of the region. The entrance to the sanctuary lies across the bridge at Thattekkad. There are several trekking trails inside Salim Ali Bird Trail, Black Baza Trail, and Trogon Trail. A walk through the interior of the woods will be a great experience and it also helps you in spotting and identifying those birds, which otherwise for the untrained eyes of a traveller, remain invisible. The guides can be hired at Assistant Wildlife Office near the entrance gate.
